We Have Always Been Leaders

 

Traditionally, Native women are leaders in our communities. Many tribal nations have included women in their most important leadershiop roles and as honorable members of their societies. In 2018, there was an unprecedented number of Native women who ran for public office across the country. More than 50 Native American women rallied behind their communities and proudly reclaimed their traditoinal leadership roles. This includes local offices, state offices, and Congress. As many Native Nations continue to rebuild their communities, it is Native women who are leading this movement. The two notable candidates who ran and won their seats in the national spotlight are Congresswomen Deb Halaand (Laguna Pueblo) from New Mexico and Congresswomen Sharice Davids(Ho-Chunk) from Kansas.

Congresswomen Sharice Davids is the first lesbian Native American woman to be elected.
